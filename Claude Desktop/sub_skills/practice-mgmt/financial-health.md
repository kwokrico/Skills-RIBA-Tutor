# Practice Management: Financial Health (Fees, Overheads, Break-even)

## Purpose
Use for fee bids, under-feeing, loss-making projects, overhead recovery, and explaining charge-out rates.

## A. Core definitions (exam-ready)
- **Direct costs**: salary + project-specific expenses.
- **On-costs**: NI, pension, holiday, training.
- **Overheads**: rent, admin staff, software, PI insurance, utilities, marketing, governance, etc.
- **Net multiplier**:

\t\( \\text{Net multiplier} = \\dfrac{\\text{Fee income}}{\\text{Direct salary cost}} \\)

- **Utilisation**: proportion of time chargeable to projects.
- **Overhead recovery rate**: how much overhead must be recovered per chargeable hour.

## B. Break-even logic
### Break-even fee (concept)
- **Break-even fee** must cover: direct salary cost for the work + allocated overhead + (optionally) a margin.

### Break-even charge-out rate (concept)
- Determine annual **recoverable hours** per person (allow for leave, admin, CPD).
- Allocate overhead to recoverable hours; add salary+on-costs; add margin.

## C. Profit margin framing
- **Why profit erodes**: scope creep, poor change control, optimistic programme, senior staff doing junior tasks, rework, client indecision.
- **Defensive position**: clarify assumptions, deliverables, exclusions, and change-control mechanism.

## D. Fee bid justification template (use as a paragraph)
**Fee basis**
- Scope: [what is included]
- Deliverables: [list]
- Programme assumptions: [list]
- Resourcing: [roles + hours]
- Risk/contingency: [allowance]
- Exclusions: [list]
- Change control: “Any material change to scope/programme will require fee adjustment and written instruction.”

## E. Loss-making project diagnosis checklist
- **Evidence**: timesheets vs fee profile, change log, client instructions, RFIs, meeting minutes.
- **Root causes**: under-fee, uncontrolled variations, programme compression, rework, unclear brief.
- **Immediate controls**: freeze scope, confirm instruction routes, issue early warning, re-baseline programme/fees.
- **Professional risk**: if continuing without instruction/fee adjustment increases exposure, flag and document.
