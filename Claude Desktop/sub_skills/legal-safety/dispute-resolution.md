# Legal & Safety: Dispute Resolution Roadmap (UK Construction)

## Purpose
Use when a scenario reaches deadlock (client vs contractor, payment/time/defects) and you must advise the next step: negotiation, mediation, adjudication, arbitration, or litigation.

## A. Triage questions (fast)
- What is the **issue type**: payment/valuation, time/EOT, defects/quality, design responsibility, termination?\n- What is the **urgency**: cashflow freeze, works stopped, reputational risk?\n- What does the **contract provide** for dispute resolution?\n- What evidence exists: notices, instructions, programmes, valuations?\n\n## B. Route overview (exam-safe)
### Negotiation (first response)
- Preserve relationship; clarify facts and contractual mechanism; propose without-prejudice meeting.\n
### Mediation
- Voluntary, confidential, pragmatic settlement with mediator; good where relationship matters.\n
### Adjudication
- Fast statutory-style pathway commonly used for construction disputes (especially payment/time).\n- Focus on evidence and contractual mechanism; decision is temporarily binding in practice.\n
### Arbitration
- Private tribunal; often used where contract provides; slower/more formal than adjudication.\n
### Litigation (Technology and Construction Court)
- Formal court route; higher cost/time; used where complex legal issues or enforcement required.\n\n## C. Evidence pack checklist (what you prepare)
- Contract/appointment and amendments.\n- Notices (delay/payment), instructions, change logs.\n- Programmes and progress records.\n- Valuations, pay less/payment notices, certificates.\n- Photos, inspection reports, snag/defect logs.\n- Meeting minutes and contemporaneous emails.\n\n## D. Output template
