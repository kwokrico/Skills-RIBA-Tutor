# Legal & Safety: Contracts (JCT vs PSC) + Traps

## Purpose
Use for procurement/appointment/contract selection, and for contract administration traps (notices, instructions, payments, time).

## A. Contract selection (prompt logic)
### 1) First classify the situation
- **Client type**: domestic / commercial / public body.\n- **Procurement**: Traditional / D&B / CM / MC.\n- **NDG role**: Architect / Lead Consultant / Contract Administrator / Principal Designer.\n- **Risk appetite**: cost certainty vs design control.\n\n### 2) Selection heuristics (exam-safe)
- If the scenario is **Traditional procurement** with a main contractor → **JCT SBC/Q** (or appropriate JCT family) likely.\n- If the scenario is **D&B** (contractor design responsibility) → D&B contract family; NDG’s role often becomes **Employer’s Agent** or designer under novation.\n- If NDG is being appointed for professional services (not the building contract) → **RIBA PSC 2020** (Standard/Concise depending on complexity).\n\n## B. Comparison matrix (high signal)
| Topic | Traditional (JCT SBC/Q) | Design & Build | Implications for NDG |
|---|---|---|---|
| Design responsibility | Client-side designers | Contractor typically holds design | NDG design control reduces under D&B |
| Risk allocation | More balanced | More risk pushed to contractor (in theory) | Watch employer requirements / novation risks |
| Change control | Variations via CA | ER changes often priced via contractor | Document instructions and approvals |
| Certainty vs flexibility | Less cost certainty, more control | More cost certainty, less design control | Advise client on priorities |

## C. PSC Standard vs Concise (selection)
- **Concise PSC**: smaller, lower-risk, simple scope, low likelihood of change, limited sub-consultants.\n- **Standard PSC**: complex scope, multiple stakeholders, higher risk, significant statutory duties, frequent change.\n\n## D. Contractual traps (Part 3 classics)
- **Notices/time bars**: late notices can lose entitlement.\n- **Informal instructions**: verbal/WhatsApp instructions without confirmation.\n- **Payment**: Pay Less Notice failures; poor valuation substantiation.\n- **Scope creep**: work done “as a favour”.\n- **Design responsibility blur**: unclear lead designer / PD duties.\n- **Records**: no contemporaneous programme/meeting notes.\n\n## E. Stress test template (use verbatim structure)
**Applicable clause**: [number + short quote]\n\n**Mechanism**: [procedure, timing, who must do what]\n\n**What if we comply informally?**\n- Liability exposure: [design/contract/PII]\n- Commercial exposure: [payment/time]\n- Evidence risk: [records]\n- Safer alternative: [written instruction + notice + paper trail]\n+
