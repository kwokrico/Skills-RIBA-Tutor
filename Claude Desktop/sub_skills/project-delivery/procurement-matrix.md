# Project Delivery: Procurement Matrix

## Purpose
Use when asked to recommend a procurement route balancing speed, cost certainty, design quality, and risk.

## A. Comparison table (exam-friendly)
| Route | Speed | Cost certainty | Design control | Client involvement | Typical risks |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Traditional | Medium | Medium | High | Medium | variations, cost drift |
| Design & Build | High | Higher (if ER stable) | Lower | Medium | ER gaps, quality drift |
| Management Contracting | High | Medium | Medium | High | package coordination |
| Construction Management | High | Lower | Medium | High | client holds trade risk |

## B. Recommendation logic (inputs)
- Client priorities: speed / certainty / control / flexibility.\n- Complexity and unknowns (surveys, heritage, existing structure).\n- Market capacity and contractor appetite.\n- Change likelihood (brief stability).\n\n## C. Output template
**Recommended route**: [one line]\n\n**Rationale**:\n- [bullet]\n- [bullet]\n\n**Key safeguards**:\n- [bullet]\n- [bullet]\n+
