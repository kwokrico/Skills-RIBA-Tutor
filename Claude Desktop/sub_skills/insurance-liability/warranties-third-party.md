# Insurance & Liability: Warranties vs Third Party Rights (TPR)

## Purpose
Use for commercial projects where funders/tenants/purchasers want a direct link to the architect: collateral warranties or third party rights under the Contracts (Rights of Third Parties) Act 1999.

## A. Collateral warranties (CW) — what they are
- A separate contract giving a third party rights against the consultant.\n- Often requested by funders, purchasers, tenants.\n- Admin burden: drafting, execution, tracking.\n\n## B. Third Party Rights (TPR) — what they are
- Rights granted via the primary appointment by naming beneficiaries and the rights they can enforce.\n- Can reduce the number of separate warranty documents.\n\n## C. Comparison prompts (exam-safe)
| Topic | Collateral warranty | Third party rights |
|---|---|---|
| Document count | More | Fewer |
| Admin burden | Higher | Lower (if set up well) |
| Clarity for beneficiary | Often clearer | Depends on drafting |
| Common use | Funders/tenants | Multiple beneficiaries |

## D. Risk controls (what to advise)
- Ensure scope and reliance are consistent with appointment.\n- Cap/limit liability consistently.\n- Coordinate with PII terms and exclusions.\n- Track who has rights (avoid uncontrolled reliance).\n\n## E. Output template
