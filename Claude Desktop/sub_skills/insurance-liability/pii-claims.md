# Insurance & Liability: PII Claims (Claims-made, Run-off, Notifications)

## Purpose
Use when scenarios involve mistakes, missed notices, design errors, or client threats, and you must decide whether/how to notify brokers/insurers.

## A. Core concept: “claims-made” (what to say)
- PII is commonly written on a **claims-made basis**: what matters is when the **claim/circumstance is notified**, not just when the work was done.\n- Late notification can prejudice cover.\n\n## B. Run-off cover (prompt)
- If practice closes, merges, or changes entity, consider **run-off** so historic work remains insured.\n\n## C. Notification decision checklist (exam-safe)
Notify/seek broker advice where:\n- A written allegation is made (letter before action, threat of claim).\n- A material defect/error is discovered with potential third-party loss.\n- A contractual/commercial event could escalate into a claim (e.g., significant delay/termination blamed on NDG).\n\n## D. What to record (defensive)
- Date and description of circumstance.\n- Parties involved.\n- Key documents/emails.\n- What immediate mitigation steps were taken.\n- Avoid admissions of liability; stick to facts.\n\n## E. Output template
**PII exposure**: [one paragraph]\n\n**Recommendation**:\n- [bullet: notify broker / seek advice]\n- [bullet: preserve evidence]\n\n**Do / don’t**:\n- Do: keep contemporaneous records; communicate factually.\n- Don’t: admit liability; conceal; delay notification.\n+
